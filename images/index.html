Chameleon

Interesting Facts about Chameleon

  1. Chameleon are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  2. Changing skin colour is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Pigeon

Interesting Facts about Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incridibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities and are highly sociable bird.
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Camel

Interesting Facts about Camel

  1. The scientific name for a camel is “Camelus”,
  2. Although there are still wild camels roaming around in parts of the world, camels became domesticated around 5,000 years ago,
  3. People in the Middle East use camels like horses. They ride them and use them to carry things. Camels can carry up to 200 pounds!

Teddy Bear

Interesting Facts about Teddy Bear

  1. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  2. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  3. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss who is known for her minuscule work.

Panda

Interesting Facts about Panda

  1. They have great camouflage for their environment.
  2. They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food
  3. At 5 months old, giant pandas learn how to climb - sometimes practicing by climbing on their mum.

Flying-Cicada

Interesting Facts about Flying-Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  2. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  3. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.
